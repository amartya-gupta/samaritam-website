What Samaritam analyses
Seven lenses. One clear picture.

Political Lean

Where does the article sit on the political spectrum? From far-left to far-right, mapped on a visual scale using Moral Foundations Theory.

Economic Axis

Does the article favour state intervention or free-market approaches? Economic framing is often where political lean hides most effectively.

Social Axis

Progressive or traditional? Where the article sits on social values, independent of its economic stance.

Rhetorical Temperature

Is the language designed to inform or to provoke? Charged words are flagged inline so you can see exactly where emotion replaces evidence.

Framing Bias

Who gets quoted? Whose perspective dominates? Whose voice is absent? Framing is the invisible architecture of every article.

Factual Density

What ratio of the article is verifiable claims versus opinion or inference? A single number that says a lot.

Headline vs Body

Does the headline fairly represent what the article actually says? Sensationalist headlines on measured reporting are one of the most common forms of bias.
