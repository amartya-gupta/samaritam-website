About Samaritam

Truth told
in balance.

Samaritam is a free tool that analyses news articles for political bias, emotional manipulation, framing patterns, and factual integrity — grounded in peer-reviewed academic research, not opinion.

Most bias-checking tools rate entire news outlets. Samaritam rates the specific article in front of you — because even a generally centrist outlet can publish a biased op-ed. The difference matters.

The Academic Framework

Samaritam's analysis is explicitly grounded in three bodies of peer-reviewed research. The AI is not giving its personal opinion — it is applying frameworks that have been tested and validated by independent researchers.

Moral Foundations Theory (Jonathan Haidt, NYU) identifies six moral foundations — care, fairness, loyalty, authority, sanctity, and liberty — that underpin political thinking across cultures. Left-leaning thought tends to weight care and fairness heavily; right-leaning thought distributes weight more evenly across all six. This framework is cross-cultural, peer-reviewed, and forms the backbone of Samaritam's political lean scoring.

The Political Compass Model separates ideology onto two independent axes — economic (state intervention vs free market) and social (progressive vs traditional). This prevents the reductive mistake of collapsing all political views onto a single left-right line.

NLP Political Bias Research from Stanford and MIT has identified specific linguistic markers — word choice, sentence framing, rhetorical patterns — that correlate with political lean in text. Samaritam applies these findings to flag emotional language and framing patterns.

A note on bias in the tool itself

Any tool that analyses political bias has to reckon with its own potential bias. We've tried to address this by anchoring every analysis to established academic frameworks rather than the AI's own tendencies. The prompt explicitly instructs the model to apply Haidt's Moral Foundations definitions and the Political Compass axes — not to form its own judgement.

No system is perfect. If you notice systematic skewing in Samaritam's analysis, we'd genuinely like to know. The contact details are below.

Privacy & Data

Samaritam is built with a privacy-first approach. Here is exactly what happens to your data:

Article text you paste is sent to Google's Gemini API for analysis. This is necessary for the tool to work. Google's API terms apply to that transmission. Samaritam does not see, store, or log your text anywhere else.

Your Sources data — outlet names and average scores — is stored entirely in your browser's localStorage. It never leaves your device. Clearing your browser data will erase it.

No accounts, no tracking, no cookies, no analytics. Samaritam has no idea who you are, where you are, or what you've analysed. There is no server receiving any of this information.
